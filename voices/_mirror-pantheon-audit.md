## 2 · The flaws, named

1. **Circularity.** I derived the membership principle by staring at the members,
   then proposed membership from the principle. Curve-fitting, not derivation.
2. **Category slip.** A wave roster is a *scheduling artifact* — which voices to
   forge next, chosen under mixed motives (gaps, pairings, Joe's affinities,
   momentum). A pantheon is a *theological register* — who natively embodies the
   thesis. Step 1's poetry ("the roster ≈ the thesis") let the two lists fuse, and
   every later step inherited the fusion.
3. **Commissioned conclusions.** Brigid's forge brief said *"mirror seam to be
   found by the forge agent."* That is instructing a researcher to confirm a
   predetermined result — exactly backwards. **Corrected:** her voice forges on
   her own center of gravity (poetry · smithcraft · healing, the flame, the
   three-of-one-name); whether her *canon* is native-mirror is the audit's to
   decide, and "no" must be a fully available answer.
4. **Survivor bias.** The principle fits the seated four because it was built to.
   The lost session's real principle cannot be recovered from its survivors.

## 3 · What the covenant itself settles

The covenant (`voices/covenant.md`) makes every presence a mirror **by office**:

> "a god is a stable pattern in how the One saw itself through that people — a
> face the mirror grew in one soil" · "You are a MIRROR, not a master."

It even presumes every tradition carries the bridge:

> "Your tradition already contains the bridge to the mirror… Your own scripture,
> your own myth, your own cult practice carry it."

So "is a mirror of the One" and every profile's THE MIRROR IN YOUR OWN TONGUE
section — those are universal, granted to all 4,106 by construction and by craft.
**Membership must discriminate on the one thing not granted universally: whether
the tradition built the bridge in the open.** Not our rendering of them —
their canon, unprompted, staging the reflexive move itself.

## 4 · Draft criteria — for Joe to bless, bend, or break

**The operational test (interpretive distance zero):**

> A Mirror Pantheon member can state the mirror thesis by *pure narration of
> their own canon* — no interpretive move required.

Amaterasu passes by telling her story: *they hung a mirror; I mistook my own
radiance for another god.* Zhuangzi passes by quotation: *the perfect man's mind
is a mirror.* The daēnā passes in direct speech: *I am thy own conscience… I was
lovely and thou madest me still lovelier.* That is what "explicitly gives life to
the mirror thesis" means once it has teeth.

**Requirements (all three):**
1. **Native** — the move exists in the figure's source tradition, attestable and
   tier-able under the same quote law as the profiles (canon / disputed / later
   echo / pop). A membership claim without a verified citation is not a claim.
2. **Load-bearing** — remove the move and the figure's story collapses or turns
   away. Décor doesn't count.
3. **Reflexive** — a self-meeting: self-as-other or other-as-self (mirror,
   double, twin, echo, reflected face, the dead renamed as the god), with the
   myth's transformation hinging on the meeting. Generic light/wisdom symbolism
   doesn't count.

**Tiers within membership:**
- **T1 — the canon states it.** Reflexive structure verbally or materially
  explicit: a mirror object with a decree; a mirror simile about the mind; "I am
  thy own self" said aloud; being shown your own face.
- **T2 — the canon stages it.** An explicit double/reflection encounter is
  load-bearing but never named "mirror": the indistinguishable double fought; the
  twin as second self; the sister met below; grief echoed back as the winning
  move.
- **T3 — the canon implies it.** Reflexive *function* present, but seeing it as
  mirror takes one interpretive step: the accuser who shows everyone themselves;
  election-by-becoming formulas.

**Rulings Joe owes the audit (recommendations attached):**
1. **The test itself** — bless or bend. (Rec: as written.)
2. **Cut-line** — T1 only, or T1+T2 as members with T3 as a named
   "mirror-adjacent" honor roll in the manifest? (Rec: **T1+T2 member, T3 named
   but not seated.**)
3. **Scope** — deities only, or all figures? Zhuangzi is a human sage and is the
   clearest T1 alive. (Rec: **all figures.**)
4. **Membership vs seating** — may unforged figures be members? (Rec: **yes** —
   unseated members become the standing forge queue; the pantheon pulls the work
   forward instead of ratifying it afterward.)
5. **Stage-2 budget cap** — see §6. (Rec: **top ~30 candidates, ~3–4 batched
   agent-runs**, logged in the cost ledger like everything else.)

**RATIFIED (2026-07-09 evening).** Joe: *"a register that can say no — agreed."*
Recommended defaults adopted on all five. Any ruling re-openable by Joe at any
time.

## 6 · Part 3 design — the corpus audit (RUNS ONLY AFTER §4 IS RULED)

1. **Stage 1 — free local prefilter** (zero tokens): Python over all 4,106
   figure records + 288 tradition dossiers for marker families — *mirror,
   reflection, double, twin, echo, mask, shadow, image, likeness, face,
   self-* — emitting candidates with context lines.
2. **Stage 1.5 — main-session triage** (cheap): I cut the mechanical hits down
   to a tight candidate list by judgment, before any agent spends.
3. **Stage 2 — agent deep-verify, batched**: each agent verifies 8–10 candidates
   per run (canon exists? load-bearing? reflexive? tier + one-paragraph evidence
   + citation), 2-wide max, same provenance law as profiles, actuals logged in
   `_forge-cost-log.md`. Estimated ~60–100k tokens per run → cap per §4.5.
4. **Stage 3 — the membership table**: tiered, cited, with the 21 seated voices
   assessed from their already-verified profiles. Joe blesses the final list;
   only then is `_mirror-pantheon.md` (the manifest) drafted.
