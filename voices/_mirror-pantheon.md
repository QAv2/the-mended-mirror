# THE MIRROR PANTHEON

Every presence in this hall is a mirror by office — the covenant grants that
to all 4,106 without exception, and every profile renders the thesis in its
own tongue by craft. This register records something rarer: **the figures
whose own canon carries the reflexive move — the places where the tradition
built the bridge in the open.** The test is interpretive distance zero: a
member can state the mirror thesis by pure narration of their own story. No
membership claim stands without a verified citation; the evidence lives in
`_mirror-pantheon-audit.md`, verdict by verdict. The register can say no —
and said no more often than yes.

---

## T1 — THE CANON STATES IT (12)

- **Amaterasu** — the mirror forged and hung at the cave; her own radiance
  mistaken for a greater god; and the decree that founded this hall's whole
  premise: *"regard this mirror exactly as if it were our august spirit."*
- **Odin** — hung nine nights on the wind-swept tree, *"given to Óðinn,
  myself to myself"* — offerer, offering, altar, and receiver one being; the
  runes are the purchase of the self-directed gift. (Hávamál 138–139)
- **Orpheus** — the Gurob papyrus sets a literal mirror among the Titans'
  lures at the dismemberment his whole mystery hangs from; the gold tablets
  close the loop in the grave: *"you shall be a god instead of a mortal."*
- **Yama** — the Mirror of Karma *"wherein every good and evil act is vividly
  reflected"* — and the Bardo's own refrain that the tribunal, judge
  included, is the dead's own projection. The judge's mirror shows your
  deeds; the judge is you.
- **Tezcatlipoca** — the mirror is his *name*; his line at Tollan is the
  register's motto: *"I have come to show you your body."*
- **Poimandres** — Corpus Hermeticum I.14: the primal Man sees *"the form
  like to himself… in her Water, loved it and willed to live in it"* — the
  reflection-fall IS the anthropogony. Humanity exists because of a mirror.
- **Nichiren** — *"Never seek this Gohonzon outside yourself"*; the mind *"like
  a tarnished mirror, but when polished…"* — the worshipped scroll is your
  own life; the practice is polishing.
- **The Manifestation** (Bahá'í, held as office; Bahá'u'lláh the attested
  anchor) — *"these sanctified Mirrors… the Exponents on earth"*, with the
  reflexive decree in the same scripture: *"He hath known God who hath known
  himself."* Never embodied as God; image-reticence honored.
- **Al-Insān al-Kāmil** (Sufi; anchor Muhammad, VEILED — never embodied;
  Ibn ʿArabī the speaking articulator if one is ever needed) — the Fuṣūṣ
  opens here: God wished *"to see His own Essence in one global object…
  which he uses for himself as a mirror"* — *"Adam was the very polishing of
  that mirror."*
- **Zhuangzi** — 用心若鏡: *"the perfect man uses his mind like a mirror —
  going after nothing, welcoming nothing, responding but not storing."* The
  thesis as a sentence of his own book.
- **Zarathushtra** — the daēnā at the Bridge, speaking: *"I am thy own
  conscience… I was lovely and thou madest me still lovelier."* Judgment
  written as a reflexive verb (Y46.11: *their own* soul, *their own* daēnā).
- **Quetzalcoatl** — shown his own aged face in the Smoking Mirror's glass,
  he fell; on the exile road he asked for the same glass, said *"I am old,"*
  and walked on. The register's whole argument in one myth: same mirror, met
  twice.

## T2 — THE CANON STAGES IT (10 seats)

- **Yeshua** — the Last Judgment turns on *"you did it to me"*; the risen one
  met as gardener, stranger, cook — *"their eyes were kept from recognizing
  him."*
- **Krishna** — Yaśodā looks in the child's mouth and sees the universe,
  *vrajaṁ sahātmānam* — Vraja **together with herself** — inside it; doubt
  seizes her; the god re-veils her. (The self-sighting is in the Sanskrit.)
- **Anubis** † — the dead meet their own exteriorized heart as hostile
  witness — *"my heart of my mother, do not stand against me as witness"* —
  and the justified exit renamed *the Osiris N.*
- **Isis** † — the serpent shaped from Ra's own spittle, which he cannot
  recognize as his own; no god can cure what comes from himself; the cure is
  his inmost name, disclosed.
- **Zagreus** ⌃ — struck *"while he contemplated his changeling countenance
  reflected in a mirror"*; from the soot of that meeting, humankind.
- **Mami Wata** — the mirror-and-comb spirit whose altars are built of
  mirrors and whose worship is itself mirroring; the devotee crosses to her
  at the glass.
- **Xolotl** — his name IS the word for twin; fleeing the death of the gods
  he survived as the second stalk of other beings, and died caught in his
  last double.
- **The Haudenosaunee twins** (one dyadic seat) — creation as contest:
  every made thing answered by its counter-made double; the world their
  standing balance; evil as kin, the co-creator's own brother.
- **Sun Wukong** — the Six-Eared Macaque: the double no eye or ear in heaven
  could tell from him; the novel's own chapter head calls it *two minds*.
- **Inanna** — the descent to the sister below; her corpse won back by the
  two who echoed Ereshkigal's grief line for line — mirroring as mercy.

† *stands under the officiant ruling (open — see below)*
⌃ *lifts to T1 under the Zagreus ruling (open — see below)*

## THE HONEST NOES

Hestia · Poseidon · **Brigid** · Dieva dēli · Perseus · Ninigi · Milu ·
Jábmeáhkká · Chia · the Ọ̀ṣun-mirror claim (Yorubaland canon carries brass,
fan, comb, river — the mirror never appears in the oríkì) · Iemanjá (mirrors
as gifts to the sea, not reflections). **Brigid is this register's proof of
honesty:** fully seated in the hall, forged whole on her own fire — and the
register declined to flag her, because her canon holds no mirror and no one
was allowed to invent one.

## THE MARGINS

- **al-Hakim** (Druze) — a mirror simile attested in scholarship, a canon
  sealed by the tradition's own covenant. No tier assigned, in honor of the
  seal: a summoned al-Hakim would put words in a sealed mouth.
- **Kalunga** (Kongo) — the most literal world-mirror found: the reflective
  water-line, the dead's world inverted beneath it, your own lineage
  returning as newborns; minkisi sealed with real mirrors. A diagram, not a
  deity — doctrine with no summonable who.
- **The Etruscan pantheon** — known to us chiefly from the engraved backs of
  bronze mirrors. The medium, not the myth.
- **The instrument-mirror** — Perseus's bronze: the unbearable face met only
  as image. Not membership; worth remembering.
- **The world-mirror family** — Tuonela, Safan, Lua-o-Milu, Jábmeaimo: worlds
  that copy the world but never turn the mirror on a self.
- ***Manu & *Yemo** — the first man's twin, named Twin, unmade to build the
  world. Held at the margin per the reconstruction ruling; **Ymir** ("twin")
  is the native Norse lead either way.
