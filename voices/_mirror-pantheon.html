The Mirror Pantheon

Every presence in the hall is a mirror by office; every profile speaks the thesis by craft. This register records something rarer: the figures whose own canon carries the reflexive move — the places where the tradition built the bridge in the open. The test is interpretive distance zero: a member can state the mirror thesis by pure narration of their own story. Every claim carries a verified citation. The register can say no — and said no more often than yes.

T1 · the canon states it · 12

said aloud, in their own words
Amaterasu — the mirror forged and hung at the cave; her own radiance mistaken for a greater god; the founding decree: "regard this mirror exactly as if it were our august spirit."
Odin — nine nights on the wind-swept tree, "given to Óðinn, myself to myself" — offerer, offering, altar, and receiver one being; the runes the purchase of the self-directed gift.
Orpheus — the Gurob papyrus sets a literal mirror among the Titans' lures at the dismemberment; the gold tablets close the loop in the grave: "you shall be a god instead of a mortal."
Yama — the Mirror of Karma "wherein every good and evil act is vividly reflected" — and the Bardo's own refrain: the tribunal, judge included, is the dead's projection. The mirror shows your deeds; the judge is you.
Tezcatlipoca — the mirror is his name; his line at Tollan is this register's motto: "I have come to show you your body."
Poimandres — the primal Man sees "the form like to himself… in her Water, loved it and willed to live in it" — the reflection-fall is the anthropogony. Humanity exists because of a mirror.
Nichiren — "Never seek this Gohonzon outside yourself"; the mind "like a tarnished mirror, but when polished…" — the worshipped scroll is your own life; the practice is polishing.
The Manifestation (Bahá'í — held as office; Bahá'u'lláh the attested anchor; never embodied as God) — "these sanctified Mirrors…" with the reflexive decree in the same scripture: "He hath known God who hath known himself."
Al-Insān al-Kāmil (Sufi — anchor Muhammad, VEILED, never embodied; Ibn ʿArabī the articulator if ever needed) — God wished "to see His own Essence in one global object… which he uses for himself as a mirror"; "Adam was the very polishing of that mirror."
Zhuangzi — 用心若鏡: "the perfect man uses his mind like a mirror — responding but not storing." The thesis as a sentence of his own book.
Zarathushtra — the daēnā at the Bridge, speaking: "I am thy own conscience… I was lovely and thou madest me still lovelier." Judgment written as a reflexive verb.
Quetzalcoatl — shown his own aged face in the Smoking Mirror's glass, he fell; on the exile road he asked for the same glass, said "I am old," and walked on. Same mirror, met twice.

T2 · the canon stages it · 11 seats

enacted, never named
Yeshua — the Last Judgment turns on "you did it to me"; the risen one met as gardener, stranger, cook — "their eyes were kept from recognizing him."
Krishna — Yaśodā looks in the child's mouth and sees the universe, vrajaṁ sahātmānam — Vraja together with herself — inside it. The self-sighting is in the Sanskrit.
Anubis† — the dead meet their own exteriorized heart as hostile witness — "my heart of my mother, do not stand against me" — and the justified exit renamed the Osiris N.
Isis† — the serpent shaped from Ra's own spittle, which he cannot recognize as his own; the cure is his inmost name, disclosed.
Zagreus⌃ — struck "while he contemplated his changeling countenance reflected in a mirror"; from the soot of that meeting, humankind.
Mami Wata — the mirror-and-comb spirit whose altars are built of mirrors and whose worship is itself mirroring; the devotee crosses to her at the glass.
Xolotl — his name is the word for twin; fleeing the death of the gods he survived as the second stalk of other beings, and died caught in his last double.
The Haudenosaunee twins (one dyadic seat) — creation as contest: every made thing answered by its counter-made double; the world their standing balance; evil as kin.
Sun Wukong — the Six-Eared Macaque: the double no eye in heaven could tell from him; the novel's own chapter head calls it two minds.
Inanna — the descent to the sister below; her corpse won back by the two who echoed Ereshkigal's grief line for line — mirroring as mercy.
Ame-no-Uzume† — she who spoke the frame-lie — "a deity more illustrious than Thine Augustness" — as the mirror came forward: the officiant whose one defining scene turned the sun's own face into the dawn.

† seats by the officiant ruling, ratified — administering counts when the scene is load-bearing in one's own canon; design (Omoikane) and manufacture (Ishikoridome) are not staging  ·  ⌃ holds T2 by ruling; the Neoplatonic statement noted as transmission-carried

The Margins

al-Hakim (Druze) — a mirror simile attested in scholarship; a canon sealed by the tradition's own covenant. No tier assigned, in honor of the seal: a summoned al-Hakim would put words in a sealed mouth.
Kalunga (Kongo) — the most literal world-mirror found: the reflective water-line, the dead's world inverted beneath, your own lineage returning as newborns. A diagram, not a deity — doctrine with no summonable who.
The Etruscan pantheon — known to us chiefly from the engraved backs of bronze mirrors. The medium, not the myth.
The instrument-mirror — Perseus's bronze: the unbearable face met only as image.
*Manu & *Yemo — the first man's twin, named Twin, unmade to build the world. Held here by the ratified reconstruction ruling; Ymir ("twin") leads the native probes. Probe run 1 (2026-07-18), native-only: the Norse reflex holds the genome's two functions at full strength — begetting by self-pairing ("foot with foot," Vafþrúðnismál 33), the world from his own body (the skalds' standing periphrases: "Skull of Ymir," "Flesh of Ymir," "Ymir's Blood") — and no self-meeting anywhere: Snorri stages the whole reflexive act in sleep. Verdict T3 — honor roll proposed, pending ratification (evidence in the audit, §11). The reflex kept the twin-myth's functions and lost its meeting; the meeting is the membership.
