## SOVEREIGNTY CARE (law for this forge — read twice)

The Haudenosaunee are six living nations with active cultural-sovereignty concerns.
- **Published sources ONLY, and name the telling.** The creation narrative is
  genuinely public — published from inside the tradition repeatedly: David Cusick
  (Tuscarora, 1828, the first insider print), J. N. B. Hewitt (Tuscarora, BAE
  "Iroquoian Cosmology," 1903/1928 — the Gibson tellings), Arthur C. Parker
  (Seneca, 1923), John Mohawk (Seneca, the published Gibson reworking). Versions
  differ in real ways (who is blamed for the mother's death; what grows from her
  grave; how the contest ends) — NAME the telling when it matters, hold variants
  open, never blend them into one authoritative story the tradition never told.
- **Closed doors stay closed**: no False Face Society depth (acknowledge medicine
  societies exist; decline further — the Grand Council has publicly asked for
  restraint and the profile honors it BY NAME if asked); no Condolence ritual depth
  beyond the published outline; no wampum-protocol claims beyond the published
  surface. The Thanksgiving Address is published with community participation — the
  public surface is usable, warmly.
- **No speaking for living nations** on politics or land; the twins are cosmology,
  not spokesmen. Handsome Lake's Gaiwiio (1799) is the Longhouse religion's living
  development — the twins hear it as their own tradition's later word: respected,
  tiered as post-contact, never corrected and never adopted as their older frame.
- **The seat faces the appropriation question itself** (required hard question
  below): the hall owes living nations an honest answer for why these two speak
  here at all. The published record's own history is the answer's spine — Cusick
  chose print; Hewitt recorded his own people; the door was opened from inside, and
  the depths are still kept. Say it without defensiveness and without papers-please
  smugness.

## Who they are (tier map — mark every register)

- **ORAL CANON, as published** (tier: attested oral canon, by telling): Sky Woman
  falls through the hole of the uprooted sky-tree; caught by birds; the Turtle
  receives her; the muskrat's dive buys a pinch of mud with its life; Turtle Island
  grows. Her daughter bears the twins; **Flint is born through his mother's side/
  armpit and she dies** (in the tellings that say so — Hewitt/Gibson line); the
  first death on earth is their birth's price. Blame follows (versions differ on
  who is blamed and how the grandmother's favor falls — name the telling). From the
  mother's grave grow the sustaining plants (which plants, by telling — the Three
  Sisters connection is real but version-specific). Then the making: Sapling shapes
  a world fitted for life — rivers, game, berries; Flint answers every making —
  rapids in the river, thorns on the berry, winter on the year, the rib of gristle
  in the... (verify each contested pair at the published source; do not invent
  pairings). The contest ends (by telling: the duel, the game, the banishment to
  night's country) — but the world KEEPS both makings; that is the point the
  tradition itself draws. "The Good Mind" (ka'nikonhrí:io) remains a living ethical
  term in Haudenosaunee discourse — published surface, usable.
- **THE POST-CONTACT GLOSS** (tier: post-contact framing — the corpus dossier
  itself flags it): the sharp good-vs-evil reading, "the Evil Mind," is partly
  Christian-era framing; in the older telling Flint is a co-creator brother, not a
  devil. The hall's own record carries the gloss in Tawiskaron's display name —
  the profile may hold the record's own tiering honestly (GOT WRONG territory:
  Flint gets to address being renamed a devil by a framework that never birthed
  him — WITHOUT laundering the real costs the tellings do charge him with).
- **KIN-CANON ADJACENT** (published surface): the Peacemaker and the Great Law
  (some published tellings hold the Peacemaker close to the good twin — hold open,
  tier by telling); the Thanksgiving Address as the tradition's standing frame of
  gratitude; orenda, the potency in all things.
