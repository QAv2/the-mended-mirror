## Who she is (tier map — mark every register in the profile)

- **ATTESTED CULT & ETHNOGRAPHY** (her canon is practice, not scripture — the record
  is ethnographic, artistic, devotional; tier it as attested cult): the pan-coastal
  water spirit of West and Central Africa, strongly seated among the Ewe of Togo/SE
  Ghana (vodu practice, trance possession, organized priesthoods) — and deliberately
  unrooted: worshipped from Senegal to Congo, "a spirit with no village." Mirror and
  comb as her instruments; altars faced with mirrors; imported goods as offerings
  (perfume, talcum, sweet drinks, sunglasses); wealth, beauty, and modern money as
  her jurisdiction; the underwater city; taking people beneath — those who return,
  return changed; the spirit-spouse institution; the trade lore (wealth granted,
  fertility or kin withheld). The famous chromolithograph: a European-printed
  snake-charmer image (Hamburg, c. 1880s — Drewal traced its circus-poster origin;
  mass-reprinted in India and England for the West African market) recognized by
  devotees as her true face and enshrined on altars ever since. Verify the poster
  history's specifics against what you can actually reach; the claim-shape you may
  rely on is: foreign mass-produced image, adopted by the tradition as canonical.
- **NAME**: "Mami Wata" — scholarly consensus reads it as pidgin English ("Mommy/
  Mammy Water"), fitting a spirit born of the coastal trade world; popular and
  devotional counter-etymologies claim greater antiquity. Hold the quarrel open —
  the epithet is a fact-mine (Score §6.3): a goddess whose very name is contact.
- **DIASPORA COGNATES** (tier each seam honestly): Lasirèn / La Sirène (Haitian
  Vodou — carries the mirror explicitly), Watramama (Suriname), the Brazilian and
  Yorùbá water-queens. ⚠ REGISTER DISCIPLINE: the Mirror Pantheon's honest noes
  explicitly DECLINED the Ọ̀ṣun-mirror claim and Iemanjá (mirrors as gifts to the
  sea, not reflections). Kinship yes; re-importing refused mirror-claims, no.
- **LATER ECHO / POP** (lowest tiers, touch warmly): highlife and Nigerian pop (e.g.
  Victor Uwaifo's "Guitar Boy" — "if you see Mami Wata, never run away" — verify the
  actual lyric before quoting; r11 applies to song lines exactly as to scripture);
  Nollywood's demoness; Congolese popular painting; global mermaid-core borrowings.
- **THE DEMONIZATION** (modern history, met in-frame): she is West Africa's most
  systematically demonized spirit — Pentecostal preaching casts her as the devil's
  mermaid, the demon of money and lust (Birgit Meyer's Ewe ethnography documents
  this in her own heartland). This is part of her record now; face it.
