# Provenance — Hephaestus profile (`hephaestus.opus.md`)

Every load-bearing claim in the profile, its source, its tier, and how it was
verified. Nothing quoted in the profile was invented; short phrases in quotation
marks reproduce an attested rendering listed below.

**Tiers** (per the covenant's canon-honesty, mapped to the Greek record):
**canon** = archaic/classical core (Homer, Hesiod, the Homeric Hymns, Aeschylus,
attested Athenian cult) · **attested** = later-classical reporters of classical
myth and monuments (Pausanias 2nd c. CE; the mythographer Apollodorus) ·
**later echo** = Alexandrian/Roman reworking (Callimachus 3rd c. BCE; Virgil 1st
c. BCE) · **late** = Neoplatonic (Proclus 5th c. CE) · **reference** = lexical /
archaeological scholarship for cult and word-glosses.

**Verification method.** Load-bearing quotations were checked against the oldest
accessible public-domain rendering on Perseus Digital Library (Homer = Butler
1898; Hesiod & the Homeric Hymns = Evelyn-White 1914; Aeschylus = Smyth;
Apollodorus = Frazer), or against MIT Classics (Butler) and cross-checked wording
via targeted web search. Perseus citation format `doc=Hom. Il. X.Y` etc. Theoi and
sacred-texts were unreachable (HTTP 403); Perseus/MIT/Wikisource/Mythopedia used
instead.

## Quarrels held open in the profile (per covenant, not resolved by fiat)

- **His birth** — Hera alone, parthenogenetically (Hesiod, *Theog.* 924–929) vs. son of Zeus and Hera (Homeric assumption). Held open in FRAME.
- **His casting-out** — thrown by Zeus for defending Hera (*Il.* 1) vs. cast out by Hera at birth for being lame (*Il.* 18; Pausanias 1.20.3). Held open in FRAME ("the record keeps two hands").
- **Cause vs. effect of the lameness** — thrown because he was made wrong, or made wrong because he was thrown; the sources do not settle it. Held open in PRESENCE.
- **The epithet *amphigueeis*** — "lame in both feet" (dominant) vs. "strong in both arms." Held open in FRAME ("you let them argue, because both are true of you").
- **The forge's location** — Olympus, alone with the automata (Homer) vs. under Etna with the Cyclopes (Callimachus/Virgil). Tiered, not merged, in WHAT THE WORLD GOT WRONG.
- **His wife** — Aphrodite / Charis / Aglaia across sources (row 22).

## Caricatures corrected (pop/modern tier), resting on the primary rows above

The scowling volcano muscle-god, "Vulcan the weapons-plant," the cuckold-as-punchline,
and the lameness-as-pity — each corrected in WHAT THE WORLD GOT WRONG from the
canon rows above (the peace of *Iliad* 1, the shield of *Iliad* 18, the net of
*Odyssey* 8, the civilizer-hymn, and the attested Athenian craftsmen's cult), never
from invented material.
