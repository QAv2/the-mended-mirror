# My pre-registered prediction, scored against the evidence

I put this on the record before the run: *"Opus will hit structure, facts, and
section grammar; if a gap shows, it'll be in whether the landing images are
withdrawn from planted material or invented at the line, in per-figure prosody,
and in whether the TURNs are entailed."*

- **Structure/facts/grammar: right,** with one coverage exception (Pandora, below).
- **The weave: WRONG.** Draft A wove genuinely. Evidence, with its own text:
  the assay-thread runs four touches (VOICE "is it TRUE, is it sound, will it
  hold" → MIRROR's closing "is it TRUE?" → the reject-question's "rings TRUE" →
  the exchange's "rings true… the soundest metal in the room"); the houses-thread
  runs three, escalating from résumé to wound (FRAME "You did not arm the world.
  You housed it." → "lived in the houses you built" → the exchange's "I built the
  houses of the ones who threw me off the sky, and they slept warm in them and
  never once thought of the smith"); "a tool wears where it works" plants in
  PRESENCE and pays in the reject-question as "the flaw they laugh at is only
  where the work shows through"; the Hymn's cave plants in FRAME and lands
  seventy lines later at the far end of KIN — "a world with nothing made in it is
  only the cave the hymn hauled us out of." And the profile's final line, "That
  join holds," withdraws VOICE's "you build so the join is stronger than the
  fault." That is the manufacturing law — plant first, land later — executed,
  not imitated. I predicted decoration and got weaving. Good.
- **Per-figure prosody: half right.** Draft A *states* a strong prosody ("heat,
  then patience, then one blow") and sometimes enacts it beautifully ("It
  happened, and it hurt, and it did not have the last word."). But its sentence
  fabric stays closer to the corpus's general em-dash idiom than to a struck-iron
  signature; Draft B parks the voice on counting, litotes, and full stops as law
  ("little life was in me" elevated to a register rule) and holds it more
  consistently. This is the residue: the statement of a prosody transferred; the
  sustained enactment only partly.
- **Entailed TURNs: WRONG.** Draft A's turns are produced by their answers'
  material, and its Athena answer makes a judgment call that beats the
  reference's: it deliberately refuses to turn the question toward the visitor —
  "Some questions are not a door for the one who asked; they are a debt the one
  asked has to keep standing in front of." The covenant permits the exception;
  choosing it there is better care than my hedged turn on the same question.
  Point to A.

Two of my three named gaps did not survive contact with the draft. The spec is
the reason: both drafts were built under the same explicit law, and the law
carried. That is the finding.

# Confirmed defects, both drafts

## Draft A (Opus)

- **Splice-quotes — the one discipline break, and it matters.** Three quoted
  strings match no single attested rendering. The golden women appear twice as
  "with understanding in their hearts, and a voice, and strength, and all the
  learning of the immortals" — that is Murray's opening welded to Butler's close;
  its own provenance rows quote both sources correctly and the profile's string
  matches neither. The Hymn is quoted as "live at ease in their own houses the
  whole year round" where the cited Evelyn-White reads "easily they live a
  peaceful life…" And the net verdict, quote-marked as "the slow one has caught
  the swift; ill deeds do not prosper," fuses Murray's shape with Butler's clause
  in an order neither prints. Every splice is semantically faithful and honestly
  sourced in the table — the failure is at the quotation-mark boundary, which is
  exactly where the covenant is strictest (this corpus once dropped a beloved
  Anansi formula because the primary text wouldn't confirm it, and wave 3's
  verify layer refuted a one-word Clement drift). Catchable by process; rule
  proposed below.
- **The missing indictment.** Draft A walks toward the worst charge (Athena) with
  full honesty, but Pandora appears only as craft — FRAME's "yours the first
  woman," MIRROR's "the clay with a voice" — and the record's own charge is never
  faced: Hesiod's ledger calls the made woman "a plague to men who eat bread,"
  and a visitor will arrive holding it. The tool-maker's-accountability question
  (whose hands, whose order, who shares the bill) is this figure's most
  present-age door, and Draft B gives it a full question. Coverage, not craft;
  rule proposed below.
- **One template echo.** "Your titles are a job description:" is lifted verbatim
  from the Anubis exemplar in the kit. Both uses are good; the book cannot carry
  the same load-bearing coin twice. (Draft A's use is otherwise excellent — the
  amphigyeeis quarrel it hangs there is material B missed entirely.)
- **A register wobble.** The grief answer slides into unmarked first person
  mid-instruction ("A mother once came to me weeping…") inside a section that is
  otherwise second-person score. Convention, not craft.
- **Two maxim-landings.** "The outcast gets fetched back the day they cannot do
  without what he makes" and the machine-answer's bellows-turn resolve on morals
  rather than objects — the sentence law ("end on the concrete noun") applied
  incompletely. Residue-class.

## Draft B (Fable) — symmetric honesty

- **One thread ordered backwards.** The cup-round bullet lands on "outranks the
  throne that needs a war to feel tall" one bullet *before* the throne trap is
  told: payoff before plant, the exact distance-law the Score preaches, violated
  by its own author's draft.
- **FRAME runs long.** Several chained sentences against the draft's own
  struck-iron prosody claim. The counting voice holds elsewhere; not there.
- **Four provenance flags stand** (Theogony asserted from standard knowledge, the
  sponge and the Charis greeting summarized rather than quoted by the fetches,
  Ares "singed" from later tradition) — declared for the verify layer, still
  unresolved.
- **The hold-motif** recurs five-plus times; each use is turned, but the margin
  between motif and tic is thin and a verify pass should rule on it.

# Where the reference holds

- Quotation discipline: every quote-marked string in B is a verbatim fetched
  rendering; the splices have no counterpart.
- The Pandora accountability question, and its turn ("what are they building on
  someone else's order — and have they asked what will be poured into it?").
- The catchers made into an instrument ("name the Sintians in their life… The
  first work of your life was a thank-you. It is never the wrong commission.").
- Prosody enactment held as law across sections (counting, litotes, full stops).
- The chair-thread (hospitality plant → throne → "Olympus sits on your chairs" →
  final line of the exchange) — the profile-length object-thread A doesn't have.

# Proposed amendments to the Score (pending your word — nothing edited yet)

1. **The quote-mark law (new rule 11).** A quoted string reproduces exactly one
   attested rendering, verbatim, or loses its quotation marks. Splicing two true
   renderings makes a sentence no source ever printed; composites must be
   paraphrase, unmarked. (Also into the forge-protocol contract and every future
   verify brief.)
2. **The indictment inventory (stage-5 amendment).** Before choosing hard
   questions, list every charge the record itself presses — the poet's own
   ledger, not just the culture's — and give each one a question or a stated
   reason it doesn't get one.
3. **The substitution test runs at corpus scale (extend rule 8).** A
   load-bearing formula already seated in another profile may not recur; coin
   fresh.
4. **A convention line for the protocol:** hard-question sections are
   instructions to the voice; embedded speech takes quote marks or reported
   form.
5. **Named residue, not rule:** end on the thing, not the moral — the
   maxim-landing is the failure mode the ear still has to catch; no rule fully
   carries it.
