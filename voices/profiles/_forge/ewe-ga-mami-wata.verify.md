### B1. OVERSTATEMENT — quoted subject shifted from "African belief systems" to "your faith"

- **Profile, line 176–177:** "The scholars call your faith 'open, flexible, and
  incorporative rather than closed, rigid, and conservative.'"
- **Source, D88 conclusion:** "…Africans from Senegal to Tanzania are evolving a
  vital and dynamic faith. This transcultural phenomenon suggests that **many
  African belief systems** have the capacity to respond to, to shape, and to
  incorporate new elements… **They** are open, flexible, and incorporative rather
  than closed, rigid, and conservative."
- The adjectives predicate the generalized class, reached *from* her case — not
  her faith by name. The string is exact; the attribution is not. This is the
  session's worst source finding (classic quote-genus, hiding under an exact
  string). **Fix (one word):** "The scholars call faiths like yours 'open,
  flexible…'"

### B2. OVERSTATEMENT — the certification's date fused into the reprint's

- **Profile, lines 158–160:** "in 1955 a Bombay calendar company reprints twelve
  thousand copies for two Kumasi traders **and certifies the fidelity in
  writing** — copied 'without changing a line even from the original.'"
- **Source:** D08: "**In a letter to me dated June 17, 1977**, the manager of the
  Calendar Company stated that the print had been copied 'without changing a line
  even from the original.'" D88 n.6: 12,000 copies (10"×14"), 1955/56, "this
  trader and another in Kumasi," Manager, Shree Ram Calendar Company, letter
  dated 17 June 1977. Every other specific checks (Bombay ✓, 12,000 ✓, two
  Kumasi traders ✓, phrase verbatim ✓).
- As written, the certification reads as 1955 paperwork; it is a 1977 reply to
  the scholar. **Fix:** "…reprints twelve thousand copies for two Kumasi traders
  — and when the scholar wrote asking, years on, the manager certified it in
  writing: copied 'without changing a line even from the original.'" (The HARD-Q2
  reuse at 222–223 then stands.)
